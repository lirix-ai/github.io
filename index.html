About

Background

My trajectory toward software development has been shaped by early exposure to Apple's product ecosystem, reinforced by iterative self-directed experimentation, and strengthened by interdisciplinary experiences that emphasize precision, responsibility, and sustained practice.

Early exposure and identity formation

Receiving an iPod mini and later watching the iPhone keynote introduced me to technology as an integrated experience: hardware, software, and communication working together to shape user behavior. In response, I began producing early technology videos and exploring the iOS simulator through the Apple Developer Program. Although these efforts were not professionally significant, they were developmentally important because they established a sense of belonging to the world of builders and creators.

Experimentation and delayed persistence

My early attempts did not result in a substantive software release beyond a simple soundboard, and I redirected effort toward sports and other structured activities. This shift illustrates a common pattern in skill acquisition: when feedback cycles are long and outcomes are uncertain, individuals often prefer domains with clearer near-term reinforcement. Despite that detour, the underlying motivation to understand and construct systems remained.

Re-entry through automation

I re-engaged with building through Shortcuts, where automation provided an accessible entry point and rapid feedback. Over time, the work evolved from isolated workflows to questions of software design: how to convert functionality into a scalable application, how to maintain reliability, and how to move from personal utility to distributable product. This progression marks a shift from tinkering to intentional engineering.

Interdisciplinary foundations

Experiences as a licensed insurance producer and as a private pilot, alongside college study in aeronautical engineering and maintenance, required accuracy, procedural discipline, and accountability. These domains contributed transferable habits relevant to development, including attention to constraints, risk management, and continuous learning within regulated or safety-conscious environments.

Current commitment

I now dedicate extensive time to development and treat the work as a long-term practice. The central driver is not novelty alone, but the compounding value of learning: each project reveals new technical and design limitations, and each limitation becomes a prompt for further improvement. In this sense, my path reflects a sustained movement from early fascination to disciplined execution.

Principles

Operating principles

The goal is to offer the inverse of common failure modes in consumer software: neglected feedback, unresolved defects, and support that is effectively inaccessible. The standard is simple: build tools that respect users' time, attention, and trust.

Feedback as an input, not a formality

User feedback is treated as a primary source of product direction and quality assurance. If you encounter an issue, reaching out directly is the fastest path to a fix and often prevents recurring problems for others. Messages are reviewed and acted on.

Where support goes

Subscriptions are reinvested into development: maintenance, performance work, accessibility improvements, and new features. Pricing is intended to remain lightweight and sustainable.

Product commitments

  • Native, polished experiences with predictable behavior
  • Interfaces that prioritize clarity over engagement tactics
  • No dark patterns, coercive prompts, or infinite feeds
  • Direct human support with follow-through

Your support funds ongoing maintenance and improvement.

How I work

From idea to App Store

The workflow is designed to reduce wasted complexity and increase product reliability.

Explore

Identify a real constraint (time, friction, confusion). Define the user, the task, and the smallest version that meaningfully solves it.

Build

Implement in SwiftUI with an emphasis on responsiveness, clarity, and predictable state. Test on real devices early and often.

Ship & Improve

Release, collect feedback, and iterate. Maintenance is treated as a continuing responsibility, not a post-launch afterthought.

Standards

Approach to quality

Quality is treated as a combination of correctness, usability, and durability. The goal is not only to ship features, but to ship software that remains stable and understandable over time.

Process

  • Build natively with SwiftUI for performance and platform consistency
  • Test on physical iPhones and iPads (not simulators alone)
  • Validate accessibility: dynamic type, contrast, VoiceOver, and tap targets
  • Iterate based on real usage patterns and user feedback
  • Fix defects quickly and document recurring root causes
