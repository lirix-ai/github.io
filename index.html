About

A practical philosophy of iteration

Lirix is built around rapid learning loops: prototype, validate, refine. We aim to ship improvements thoughtfully, while staying flexible as real-world feedback arrives.

We prefer clear interfaces, consistent patterns, and responsible defaults. When a decision is uncertain, we choose the option that preserves user control and reduces surprises.

What we optimize for

  • Legible, accessible UI that feels at home on iOS and macOS.
  • Simple workflows that reduce cognitive load.
  • Incremental releases that can be measured and adjusted.
  • Respectful handling of user data and platform permissions.

Quality

A rapid design and iteration process

We iterate quickly, but not recklessly. Changes are designed to be reversible, understandable, and considerate of accessibility and platform conventions.

How we iterate

  • Start with a narrow problem statement and a minimal flow.
  • Review for accessibility: text size, contrast, focus order, and reduced motion.
  • Test core paths and edge cases; refine copy and empty states.
  • Ship incremental updates and improve based on user feedback.

Privacy

Privacy-first defaults

By default, we do not collect personal data. Some optional features or third-party services may process limited information to function (for example, to deliver content or provide diagnostics), and we aim to be transparent about those cases.

No data collected by default

Unless a feature explicitly asks you to provide information, our apps are designed to function without collecting personal data.

  • No mandatory account creation.
  • No default tracking for advertising purposes.
  • Clear permission requests when needed.

Third-party services

Some apps may integrate third-party services (for example, to process payments, deliver content, or provide crash diagnostics). If used, those services may receive limited data necessary to operate.

  • Service providers may process device or usage signals for reliability.
  • Where feasible, processing is minimized and scoped to the feature.
  • We avoid unnecessary sharing and prefer privacy-respecting options.

Possible data processing

Depending on the app and enabled features, data processing may include:

  • Crash logs or diagnostic reports to improve stability.
  • Feature usage signals to understand broad usability issues.
  • Content you voluntarily submit (for example, support messages).
