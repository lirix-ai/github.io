About

A practical philosophy of iteration

Lirix App Publishing is built around rapid learning loops: prototype, validate, refine. We aim to ship improvements thoughtfully while staying flexible as real-world feedback arrives.

We prefer clear interfaces, consistent patterns, and responsible defaults. When a decision is uncertain, we choose the option that preserves user control and reduces surprises.

Our apps are part of a larger vision: experiences that add enjoyment, purpose, or utility — without turning privacy into an afterthought.

What we optimize for

  • Legible, accessible UI that feels at home on iOS and macOS.
  • Simple workflows that reduce cognitive load.
  • Incremental releases that can be measured and adjusted.
  • Respectful handling of user data and platform permissions.

Quality

A rapid design and iteration process

We iterate quickly, but not recklessly. Changes are designed to be reversible, understandable, and considerate of accessibility and platform conventions.

How we iterate

  • Start with a narrow problem statement and a minimal flow.
  • Review for accessibility: text size, contrast, focus order, and reduced motion.
  • Test core paths and edge cases; refine copy and empty states.
  • Ship incremental updates and improve based on user feedback.

Privacy

Full policy

1) Scope

This policy covers (a) this website and (b) apps published under the Lirix App Publishing label on Apple platforms. Individual apps may include additional in-app notices describing feature-specific behavior. Our App Store product pages also disclose data practices through Apple’s privacy disclosures.

2) Data we collect, how we collect it, and how we use it

Apple requires privacy policies to clearly and explicitly identify what data (if any) is collected, how it’s collected, and all uses of that data, plus retention/deletion and how to revoke consent/request deletion.

  • Support communications: If you email us, we receive your email address and the contents of your message (and any attachments you choose to include). We use this to respond and troubleshoot.
  • Voluntary submissions in an app: Some apps may let you voluntarily submit text/files (for example, feedback). We use it only for the purpose shown in the feature.
  • Diagnostics (optional / app-dependent): Some apps may collect crash reports or diagnostic signals to improve stability. Where available, we prefer aggregated or minimized diagnostics.
  • Purchases: If an app offers in‑app purchases or subscriptions, Apple processes payment. We don’t receive your full payment details.

3) Third parties, sharing, and protection

We don’t sell personal data. We may share limited data with service providers only when necessary to operate a feature (for example, crash diagnostics). If we share data with a third party, we require protections consistent with this policy and Apple’s requirements.

4) Third‑party AI

If an app feature uses a third‑party AI service and would share personal data with that AI service, we will:

  • Clearly disclose what personal data is shared and where it is sent.
  • Obtain explicit permission before sharing.
  • Offer an alternative path when feasible (for example, a manual workflow or an on-device mode).

5) Consent, permissions, and withdrawing consent

  • System permissions: You can grant or revoke access (e.g., Photos, Location, Contacts) through iOS/iPadOS/macOS Settings.
  • Optional features: Where an app offers optional data-related features, you can disable them in-app when available.

6) Tracking and advertising

We do not track users for advertising by default. If any app ever implements tracking as defined by Apple, it will request permission using AppTrackingTransparency before tracking.

7) Accounts and account deletion (if applicable)

If an app supports account creation, it will also offer account deletion within the app, consistent with Apple’s requirements. If an app doesn’t include significant account-based features, we aim to let people use it without requiring a login.

8) Data retention and deletion requests

  • Support emails: We keep support correspondence only as long as needed to resolve the issue, maintain records of fixes, and prevent repeated problems. You may request deletion by emailing us.
  • Diagnostics (if used): Retention depends on the diagnostic provider/app feature, and we aim to minimize retention where possible.

Privacy contact and deletion requests: lirix.ai@proton.me

9) Security

We use reasonable safeguards designed to protect information we handle. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we aim to minimize risk by minimizing collection.

10) Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update this page and revise the “Last updated” date.
